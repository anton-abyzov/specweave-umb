### 2/ (Problem — 239 chars)

The problem isn't that AI is stupid.

It's that AI is confidently wrong.

Claude didn't say "I'm not sure." It gave me titles, years, co-authors — all fabricated with the conviction of a Wikipedia article.

### 3/ (Why — 221 chars)

Why does this happen?

LLMs predict the next most likely word. When asked about someone's papers, it generates plausible-sounding titles from patterns.

It's not lying. It's pattern-matching without verification.

### 4/ (Tips — 271 chars)

5 ways to catch hallucinations:

1. Tell AI: "Say I don't know if unsure"
2. Cross-reference: Google exact title + author
3. Ask for DOIs or URLs — fake papers won't have them
4. Prompt: "Cite your sources"
5. Ask AI to rate its confidence 1-10

### 5/ (Lesson — 243 chars)

The meta-lesson:

AI is a thinking partner, not a fact database.

Trust it for reasoning. Verify it for facts.

People who thrive with AI aren't the ones who trust it blindly — they're the ones who know when to double-check.
