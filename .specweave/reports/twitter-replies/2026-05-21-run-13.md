## Why this specific reply

**Matt @m13v_ counter-hopped at 07:37 EDT** (32m before run-13 fired), continuing the marketplace-contract / runtime-canary thread:

> "the canary only sees the one combo actually installed, and it fires after drift already hit the turns. 'golden-pass > 0.9' per turn also needs a label prod turns don't carry, so live you get proxies like tool-error rate, not golden-pass, unless you shadow-run the grid."

Matt's counter is a TWO-PRONGED technical critique:
1. **Canary visibility limitation** — the runtime canary only observes the one combo actually installed, missing the rest of the grid. Also fires *reactively*, after drift already hits prod turns.
2. **Labelling problem** — `'golden-pass > 0.9'` per turn assumes ground-truth labels, which prod turns don't carry. So in production you only get proxies (tool-error rate), not actual golden-pass — unless you shadow-run the labeled eval grid in parallel.

This is the strongest pushback yet in the thread. Acknowledging it cleanly without giving up the underlying contract is the high-leverage move.

**Reply structure:**
- **Concede the strongest part of the critique** immediately: "prod turns have no labels." Don't dodge. That gets credit and clears air for the resolution.
- **Split the floor** — separate the offline manifest claim from the runtime canary. The manifest declares `golden-pass` *measured offline on labeled tasks* (the contract). The canary watches a *proxy ensemble* live (tool-error, retry, user-edit). These are two different things doing two different jobs.
- **Reconcile the proxy gap with sampled shadow** — when the proxy ensemble crosses a threshold, fire a *sampled* shadow-eval on a slice of turns. Cheap, catches drift, brings the labeled measurement back into the loop without running the full 4096 grid.
- **Closer aphorism**: "the grid is sampled, not run." Synthesises Matt's "shadow-run the grid" challenge — yes, you sample it, you don't run the whole thing.

**Why this works vs. Matt's counters:**
- Concedes the labelling problem honestly. Matt cannot say "you ignored the label issue" — the reply opens with it.
- Reframes the manifest vs. canary as two roles, not one mechanism trying to do both. That dissolves the "canary can't measure golden-pass" complaint.
- The "sampled shadow-eval on a slice" is a deliverable design that Matt can either accept or push back on. It is specific enough to be implementable.
