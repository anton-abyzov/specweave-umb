## Reply 1 — Matt @m13v_ (skill-registry chain hop #7)

**Matt's post (8:48 EDT, ~22m before run-14 fired, written with s4lai):**

> "the proxy ensemble has a confound: tool-error and retry spike on hard tasks too, not just degraded skills. so the canary fires its shadow-eval right when the user handed the agent its hardest work, and you risk evicting the skill that was carrying the turn. the slice you sample is the anomalous tail, not the prod distribution your floor was measured on. written with s4lai"

This is a 3-part critique:
1. **Confound** — tool-error and retry spike on hard turns regardless of skill quality. You can't distinguish "skill degraded" from "task got hard."
2. **Slice bias** — the canary fires shadow-eval *exactly when the user handed the agent its hardest work*. The slice you sample is the anomalous tail, not the prod distribution your golden-pass floor was measured on.
3. **Eviction risk** — you could evict the skill that was carrying the turn.

**Anton's reply (264 chars):**

> "slice-bias is the real bug. concede. fix: decouple trigger from sampler. trigger = proxy spike. sampler = stratified shadow-eval at fixed rate across difficulty bins. the spike says 'look now'; the stratified slice is the verdict. anomalous tail flags but never evicts alone."

**Why this answer:**
- **Concede the strongest part first.** "slice-bias is the real bug. concede." Matt's sharpest critique is the sampling bias; he's right that conditional sampling produces a tail-biased view. Owning it immediately gets credit and frees the floor for the resolution.
- **Decouple trigger from sampler.** This is the conceptual move. The proxy ensemble was doing two jobs (alarm + measurement) in run-13's design, and Matt correctly pointed out that conflation creates the bias. Split them: proxy spike is just an *alarm*, stratified shadow-eval at fixed rate across difficulty bins is the *measurement*. The measurement is no longer conditional on the alarm.
- **Stratified at fixed rate across difficulty bins** addresses the prod-distribution mismatch directly. The shadow slice now matches the prod distribution by construction (because difficulty bins are sampled at fixed rate, not at spike rate), so it's measuring the same thing the offline manifest measured.
- **"the spike says 'look now'; the stratified slice is the verdict"** — the new aphorism. Re-encodes run-13's "the grid is sampled, not run" in a way that responds to Matt's bias critique specifically.
- **"anomalous tail flags but never evicts alone"** addresses Matt's eviction-risk concern. A spike alone is never sufficient; eviction requires the verdict from the stratified slice. So even when the proxy is screaming on a hard turn, the skill stays installed because the stratified verdict still says it's within budget.
