- **"drift can't hide in inputs the agent can't touch."** The aphoristic closer. Compresses the entire endogeneity-defense into 11 words. Direct inversion of Matt's "predictor absorbs the regression into its inputs."

- **"concede then split" landed seven consecutive hops (runs 12-18).** Pattern: 3-word concession naming the strongest critique phrase, then the conceptual move that dissolves it. - **Endogeneity is a universal eval pitfall, not just an agent-eval pitfall.** Any predictor trained on features that the system can influence will absorb regressions. Same problem in causal inference (treatment-influenced covariates), in betting markets (price-influenced features), in soccer xG (formation-influenced shot quality). The split exogenous/endogenous trick generalises to every monitoring pipeline. Worth a standalone post in a future "predictor design 101" thread, outside the agent-eval framing.
- **Paused branches can reactivate.** The Virgil wiki-content branch was declared paused in run-17. It came back 6 hours later with a sharper frame. Rule: don't declare a branch dead until 24h of silence. Pause states are reversible.

Showing N>1 expands the load-bearing weight of the primitive without adding complexity. Useful frame for any "what's the protocol vs. what's the policy" debate. The protocol is the primitive; the policies are UIs.
- **Tag survival is the engineering meat.** Naming concrete failure modes (copy-paste, merge, manual rewrite, format-on-save) signals implementation thinking, not architecture thinking. Lands much better with engineering audiences than abstract "we need versioning" claims.
- **Matt's cadence is accelerating.** Hop #12 came 4 minutes after Anton's hop #11. This is the fastest counter-cadence in the chain.
